List of Minisymposia

  • Advances in Discontinuous Galerkin Methods for complex wave propagation problems (Sascha Schnepp, ETH Zurich and Stephane Lanteri, INRIA)
    The Discontinuous Galerkin method has reached a state of maturity and is now widely developed for addressing various and increasingly complex problems in computational sciences. This mini-symposium is, hence, concerned with advances and novel DG techniques for the computer simulation of complex wave propagation problems. Contributions on methodological advances - such as hybridized DG methods, efficient time integration, error estimation and adaptivity - or the modeling of complex situations and materials - such as encountered in nano-sciences, bioelectromagnetics or coupled problems - are most welcome.
  • Numerical modeling of metamaterials (Jichun Li, UNLV)
    The aim of this minisymposium is to exchange ideas and further explore some major challenges and approaches to numerical modeling of wave propagation in metamaterials, including numerical studies of nonlinear metamaterials, multiscale modeling, material parameters retrieval, optimization algorithms and applications of metamaterials.
  • Coupled problems in electromagnetics (Pavel Karban, UWB)
    The aim of this minisymposium is to present and discuss state-of-the-art mathematical models, numerical methods, and computational techniques for solving multiphysics coupled problems rooted in electromagnetic fields. Target applications include induction and dielectric heating, electromechanical transducers, and high-voltage phenomena of thermoelasticity.
